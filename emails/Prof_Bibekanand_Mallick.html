Hi Bibekanand,

Your genome-wide TSCC work narrowed 406 differentially expressed piRNAs to a single causal axis — piR-33422 repressing FDFT1. That's the hard part: pulling the real driver out of a crowd of correlated non-coding signal, which is exactly what causal target discovery is for.

We built BiRAGAS to do that — take RNA-seq counts and return a causal map that separates true regulatory drivers from correlated background, with each edge traceable to the evidence behind it. On BeatAML2 it called 8 of 11 AML drug responses right and abstained on the rest rather than bluff.

There's a 51-question Help Companion if you want to poke at it: https://mayasss-gif.github.io/BiRAGAS-Help-Companion/
