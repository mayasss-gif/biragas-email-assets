Hi Weijun,

Your ScrambleBench finding — that structure-based generative models often recapitulate training-set proteins rather than genuinely learning binding-site chemistry, with high redocking RMSD and low hit rates even on CDK2/GSK3B they were trained on — is exactly the correlation-versus-causation gap we work on: separating genuine mechanistic drivers of target engagement from memorized statistical association.

That separation is the whole design premise of BiRAGAS: it scores which associations hold up as mechanistic drivers versus memorized correlation, and abstains when the evidence doesn't support a call. On the BeatAML2 cohort it retroactively called 8 of 11 AML drug responses right, and held back rather than guess on the rest.
